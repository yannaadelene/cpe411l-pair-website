Dog Breed Directory

Explore detailed information about the world's most popular dog breeds.

Golden Retriever

Weight 55 - 75 lbs
Life Span 10 - 12 Years
Temperament Devoted, Friendly
Activity Very Active

Golden Retrievers are serious workers at hunting and field work, as guides for the blind, and in search-and-rescue, enjoy obedience and other competitive events, and have an endearing love of life when not at work.

German Shepherd

Weight 50 - 90 lbs
Life Span 7 - 10 Years
Temperament Brave, Intelligent
Activity High Energy

German Shepherd Dogs are energetic and fun-loving, and they are also very fond of children once a relationship is established. They are a loyal and protective breed, often used in police and military roles.

Beagle

Weight 20 - 30 lbs
Life Span 12 - 15 Years
Temperament Amiable, Curious
Activity Moderate

There are two beagle varieties: those standing under 13 inches at the shoulder, and those between 13 and 15 inches. Both varieties are sturdy, solid, and "big for their inches."

Bulldog

Weight 40 - 50 lbs
Life Span 8 - 10 Years
Temperament Docile, Willful
Activity Low

Kind but courageous, friendly but dignified, the Bulldog is a thick-set, low-slung, well-muscled bruiser whose "sourmug" face is the universal symbol of courage and tenacity.

Poodle

Weight 45 - 70 lbs
Life Span 10 - 18 Years
Temperament Alert, Instinctive
Activity High

Whether Standard, Miniature, or Toy, the Poodle is one of the most intelligent and versatile breeds. They are eager to please and easily trained, making them favorites in dog shows.

Labrador

Weight 55 - 80 lbs
Life Span 10 - 12 Years
Temperament Kind, Outgoing
Activity High

The sweet-faced, lovable Labrador Retriever is America's most popular dog breed. Labs are friendly, outgoing, and high-spirited companions who have more than enough affection to go around.
